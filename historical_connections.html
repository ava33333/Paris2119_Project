Paris 2119 Historical Connections

The previous page initially represented the Paris 2119 government before its intentions were nefarious. The page which led you here showed how abrupt changes like this can feel, and how dangerous it can be for one group or individual to restrict information from the public. As shown in the BD, this technology was initially intended for good. It was supposed to improve the flow of traffic, make travel more accessible, and reduce emissions from vehicular transport.

However, there was a severe lack of transparency on how the technology truly functioned. This was portrayed on the unredacted version of the Transcore file. The intentional restriction of the redacted version a few seconds after the refresh reflects how the Paris 2119 government tried to sweep things under the rug to maintain their image. Tristan, who happened be at the wrong place at the wrong time, witnessed the murders of escaped duplicates, which led to the government wiping Kloe's memory, erasing Tristan's identity, and attempting to kill him for knowing too much.

Though some limitations were discussed, even the unredacted document hid the horrors in loaded bureaucratic language, which downplay the true mechanisms behind the Transcore. The widespread adoption of this technology by Paris 2119 citizens shows how little most people really know about it. This censorship also reflects the Gaullist movement during the Post World War 2 era, which censored many forms of literature, including BDs, in France.

France was occupied for a large part of the war, and Gaullism was the reaction to this. While the intentions behind the censorship were to strengthen France's image, de Gaulle's vision for a strong state was not well received by many, and led to the more radical political narratives that appeared in the late 1960s and early 1970s. This type of restistance through political commentary is also embedded in Paris 2119, as Zep presents the government and citizens as the problem, rather than that Transcore itself.

While the Transcore was the main technology highlighted in the BD, it is relevant to note that there are other big technological advancements in the world of Paris 2119, including an atmospheric disinfection program, extreme genetic modification, food molecular fabrication technology, virtual worlds, and much more. At the beginning of the story, Tristan notes how most people avoid the real world, often creating a simulated virtual world. All technology is heavily embedded in their society, and many seem to accept and rely on it for almost everything. This is a pattern that is emerging with AI, as well.

While technology, like AI, can make life much more efficient, it is important that we, as individuals, are curious about how the technology works, its side effects, and its limitations. In the BD, the government overreach and secrecy is proliferated by the overreliance on technology by the Paris 2119 citizens.
