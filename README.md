# Paris 2119 — Interactive Digital Essay

**FRCS 102 Final Project** | College of Charleston

A GitHub Pages site built as an interactive digital essay analyzing *Paris 2119* by Zep. The project explores how the bande dessinée frames technology not as inherently dangerous, but as a surface onto which power concentration, institutional secrecy, and blind trust become visible — anxieties with deep roots in French cultural history.

---

## Thesis

Technology is neutral. The danger in *Paris 2119* lies in who controls it, what they conceals, and who chooses not to question it.

---

## Site Structure

| Page / Section | Description |
|---|---|
| Ship of Theseus demo | Interactive intro exploring identity and continuity under transformation |
| Transcore mechanics | Simulation of the BD's central technology |
| French cultural history | From the Revolution through the Resistance to the *loi de 1949* |
| Imagined BD history | Glitch/redaction effect — a speculative visual layer |
