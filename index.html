Paris 2119 Project

Whether they like to travel or not, most people do not enjoy the actual act of travelling itself. The idea of sitting on a cramped bus, plane, or car for hours on end to reach your destination isn't what makes the experience, but the destination which makes that worth it. Now imagine that, instead of sitting for hours on end, you could be halfway across the world in a matter of seconds by teleporting. No traffic, no delayed flights, no waiting.

The bande desinée "Paris 2119" explores the potential complexities of a technology like this, in a society presented as a utopia. The author, Zep, presents the struggle with powerful technology as an issue of a power imbalance and lack of transparency, rather placing blame on the technology itself.

My website, which is based on an open-ended final project for my Francophone Cultures course on bande desinées at College of Charleston, is set up as an interactive demonstration of these idea. I intentionally chose to slowly develop my thesis, rather than presenting the argument outright, similar to how a French paper would be laid out.

The best way to experience this website is by clicking through each page in the order I provide below. You can also check out each page individually by navigating to the menu on the top left.

Page order:
  • Ship of Theseus demo: a demonstration on the philosophy behind the Ship of Theseus & the connections it has to the BD
  • Transcore Simulation: a demonstration on how the Transcore works and its limitations
  • MIF Archive: a page that looks like a futuristic French government page, which displays the overly authoritarian Paris 2119 government in action. Note: There is no overt button to go to the next page. You must find it.
  • Historical Connections: a page on the connections between real French/global history and the BD, as well as how the perspective this BD gives is important to discuss with the rise of powerful technology like AI
