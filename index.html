Paris 2119 Project

Welcome to my Paris 2119 Project website. Throughout the FRCS 102 course, we discussed various different bande desinées (BDs). For our final project, we got to decide what we would create based off of what we read over the semester. I chose Paris 2119, which is about a futuristic sci-fi version of Paris, and their overly secretive, authoritarian government. The website is set up as an interactive demonstration, which slowly develops my thesis, similar to how a French paper would be laid out. I used this format to argue that technology, like the Transcore, is a tool, and as a society it is essential to ensure the most ethical use of powerful technology, along with full transparency of its limitations. I chose this BD specifically because I felt that it was especially relevant with the current exponential growth of AI. The best way to experience this website is by clicking through each page in the order I provide below. You can also check out each page individually by navigating to the menu on the top left of each page.

Page order:
- Ship of Theseus demo: a demonstration on the philosophy behind the Ship of Theseus & the connections it has to the BD
- Transcore Simulation: a demonstration on how the Transcore works and its limitations
- Imagined History of the BD: a page that looks like a futuristic French government page, which displays the overly authoritarian Paris 2119 government in action.
- Historical Connections: a page on the connections between real French/global history and the BD, as well as how the perspective this BD gives is important to discuss with the rise of powerful technology like AI
